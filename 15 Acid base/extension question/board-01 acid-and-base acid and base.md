# acid and base

1. How are acids and bases defined under the Arrhenius theory?
Answer: Arrhenius acids release H⁺ in aqueous solution, while Arrhenius bases release OH⁻ in aqueous solution.

2. How are acids and bases defined under the Bronsted-Lowry theory?
Answer: A Bronsted-Lowry acid is a proton donor, while a Bronsted-Lowry base is a proton acceptor.

3. How do conjugate acid-base pairs differ?
Answer: A conjugate acid-base pair differs by one proton, H⁺.
