# Distinguishing Tests

1. After heating with NaOH(aq), acidifying with HNO₃(aq), and adding AgNO₃(aq), what precipitate colours identify CH₃Cl, CH₃Br, and CH₃I?
Answer: CH₃Cl: white ppt of AgCl.
CH₃Br: pale cream ppt of AgBr.
CH₃I: yellow ppt of AgI.

2. Using ethanolic AgNO₃ and warming, which methyl halide reacts fastest and which reacts slowest?
Answer: CH₃I reacts fastest; CH₃Cl reacts slowest.

3. How can CH₃Br be distinguished from bromobenzene using these tests?
Answer: CH₃Br gives a pale cream precipitate of AgBr, whereas bromobenzene gives no precipitate because its C–Br bond is strengthened and has partial double-bond character.
