# chlorobenzene

1. Why is the C–Cl bond in chlorobenzene strengthened?
Answer: The p orbital of Cl overlaps with the π electron cloud of the benzene ring, so the lone pair of electrons on Cl can delocalise into the benzene ring. The C–Cl bond gains partial double-bond character and is strengthened.

2. How does the benzene ring hinder nucleophilic substitution in chlorobenzene?
Answer: The π electron cloud of the benzene ring repels incoming electron-rich nucleophiles and hinders backside attack on the electron-deficient carbon.
