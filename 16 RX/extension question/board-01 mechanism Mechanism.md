# Mechanism

1. For the SN1 mechanism shown, what is formed in the slow first step?
Answer: The C–Br bond breaks heterolytically to form a planar carbocation intermediate and Br⁻.

2. Why is a racemic mixture formed in the SN1 mechanism shown?
Answer: In the 2nd step of SN1, the nucleophile can attack the sp² planar positively charged carbon from either side of the plane with equal probability to form enantiomers of equal proportion.

3. How do the rate equations differ for the SN1 and SN2 mechanisms shown?
Answer: SN1: rate = k[C₄H₉Br].
SN2: rate = k[C₄H₉Br][OH⁻].
