# Test

1. What observation is expected when a volatile small amine is warmed with NaOH(aq)?
Answer: A pungent gas is evolved and turns damp red litmus paper blue.

2. Why does a long-chain amine with 6 C or more give no pungent gas on warming?
Answer: It is not volatile and does not vaporise upon warming.

3. Why does a primary amide give a pungent gas when heated with NaOH(aq)?
Answer: The amide undergoes alkaline hydrolysis to form volatile NH₃, which vaporises.
