# Example 2

1. How does the electronegativity difference between fluorine and hydrogen affect the position of the bonding electron pairs in NF₃ compared to NH₃?
Answer: Fluorine is more electronegative than hydrogen, so it attracts the bonding electron pairs closer to itself in NF₃, whereas the bond pairs remain closer to the nitrogen atom in NH₃.

2. Explain why the repulsion between bond pairs is greater in NH₃ than in NF₃, and state its effect on the bond angle.
Answer: Because the bond pairs of electrons are closer together around the central nitrogen atom in NH₃, there is greater repulsion between these bond pairs, which makes the bond angle in NH₃ greater than in NF₃.
