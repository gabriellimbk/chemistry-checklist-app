# Relative acidity

1. How does conjugate-base stability affect acid strength?
Answer: The more stable the conjugate base, the stronger the acid.

2. Why does an electron-withdrawing Cl atom increase the acidity of a carboxylic acid?
Answer: Cl being electronegative and electron-withdrawing helps to disperses the negative charge on the carboxylate ion and therefore stabilises the conjugate base of the acid.

3. How does proximity of Cl to the -COO- group affect acidity?
Answer: A closer Cl atom gives a stronger electron-withdrawing effect, further stabilising the conjugate base and increasing acidity.
