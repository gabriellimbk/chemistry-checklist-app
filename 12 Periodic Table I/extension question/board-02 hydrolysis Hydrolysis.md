# Hydrolysis

1. Why is CCl₄ unable to hydrolyse unlike SiCl₄?
Answer: The C atom is sterically hindered by four large Cl atoms, and it does not have low-lying vacant orbitals to accept a lone pair from H₂O.

2. Why does [Al(H₂O)₆]³⁺ hydrolyse to a larger extent than [Mg(H₂O)₆]²⁺?
Answer: Al³⁺ has a higher charge density and greater polarising power, so it distorts the electron cloud of H₂O to a larger extent, and weakens the covalent O-H bond in H₂O to a larger extent.
