# Explaining Trends

1. Explain why the first ionisation energy of an element with an ns² np¹ configuration is lower than that of an element with an ns² configuration?
Answer: The p orbital is higher in energy level compared to the s orbital, hence less energy is required to remove the electron in the p orbital.

2. Why does an element with an ns² np⁴ configuration have a lower first ionisation energy than an element with an ns² np³ configuration?
Answer: The paired electron in the p orbital of the ns² np⁴ configuration experiences additional electron-electron repulsion, meaning less energy is required to remove it.

3. What is the trend for ionic radii across a period?
Answer: Across a period, ionic radii decrease across cations, show a sharp increase from cation to anion, and then decrease across anions.
